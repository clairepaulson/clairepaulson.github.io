Juvenile Remediation Curriculum

Skills

  • Articulate 360
  • Canvas LMS
  • Vyond

Client

North Carolina Division of Juvenile Justice

Audience

Juvenile defendants and support specialist trainers working 1-on-1 with them.

Project Description

As part of a team that included SMEs in juvenile justice and social work from UNC-Chapel Hill and NC A&T, I designed and developed a 10-module remediation curriculum for juvenile defendants found not competent to stand trial. The curriculum, built as part of a contract between the School of Government and the North Carolina Division of Juvenile Justice, is designed to be used in an intensive 1-on-1 facilitation setting to train these youth about the court process, what it means to be facing charges, and how to navigate the process. Challenges of this project included a tight timeline, unusual delivery setting (online materials delivered in a 1-on-1 face-to-face context), and the need to reach youth of varying ages and abilities with a single product. The final product, due to roll out across North Carolina in 2025, was built in Articulate Rise with multiple Articulate Storyline blocks and Vyond videos, and it is hosted as separate modules within a Canvas course site.

Online MPA Migration

Skills

  • Canvas LMS
  • Monday
  • HTML
  • Process Development
  • Quality Assurance
  • User Research

Client

School of Government MPA Program

Audience

MPA stakeholders including program leadership, students, lead faculty, and instructors.

Project Description

In 2024, the School of Government ended its relationship with the company 2U, who had previously managed the school’s online delivery of its Master of Public Administration (MPA) program. I led the migration of the school’s 33 online courses into our learning management system, Canvas. This process was not a simple export-import of course packages, since 2U uses multiple learning tools within Canvas to organize content that we do not use. Using qualitative user research methods and rapid prototyping, my team developed a new comprehensive Canvas course template to use for both online and residential MPA courses to improve usability for students and bolster the school’s desired “one MPA program” branding. We also undertook a thorough QA process, developed and delivered training about the migration to program students, staff, and faculty, and piloted and refined an internal course creation and major course revision process. Despite a tight timeline for the migration and a hiring freeze at the school, we delivered all courses at a higher standard than initially discussed for the initial migration.

APN Building Multidisciplinary Teams

Skills

  • DominKnow One
  • Vyond
  • Micro-credentialing tools (Canvas Badges)

Client

Adult Protection Network (APN), as part of a service agreement with North Carolina’s Division of Aging and Adult Services (DAAS)

Audience

DAAS personnel and others interested in forming multidisciplinary adult protection teams in their communities.

Project Description

The Adult Protection Network’s Building Multidisciplinary Teams course is a series of 13 modules for professionals in North Carolina communities who may want to launch adult protection multidisciplinary teams – teams that convene to address issues in their community of adult abuse or neglect. APN, an internal organization within the School of Government, contracted with my team to design and develop the course as part of a service agreement with the North Carolina Division of Aging and Adult Services. The course, a traditional interactive slide-based e-learning experience, was developed using the e-learning authoring software DominKnow One and includes talking head videos, custom-built scenarios and interactive activities, and animated videos created with Vyond. I served as the primary instructional designer working on the project, contributing the bulk of the design and development work and also managing work product from a full-time employee, multiple student workers, and an external contractor.

LELA Badging Project

Skills

  • Micro-credentialing tools (Canvas Badges)
  • EdTech evaluation and selection

Client

Center for Public Leadership and Governance at SOG (CPLG), North Carolina Association of County Commissioners (NCACC), and the North Carolina League of Municipalities (NCLM).

Audience

Local elected officials across North Carolina.

Project Description

The Local Elected Leaders Academy is a leadership program for city and county elected officials in North Carolina. To proceed through program “achievement levels,” participants must engage in both leadership courses – many offered by the School of Government – and professional service activities, such as speaking at elected official conferences or serving on committees. Prior to this project, participant progress through the program was tracked on a spreadsheet internal to one of the participating organizations, making it difficult for elected leaders to know how they were progressing. In collaboration with the CPLG, I evaluated several micro-credentialing tools for this unusual use case, selected Canvas Badges (formerly Badgr), and developed a micro-credentialing program within that tool that includes four complex and flexible learning pathways and around 100 badges. I also designed and provided training about the program at professional conferences and co-authored a conference paper about its development.

DataAware Program

Skills

  • Jupyter Notebook
  • Grant writing
  • Curriculum development

Client

Carolina Health Informatics Program (CHIP)

Audience

NC Triangle-area high school students

Project Description

In 2019, in my role as a Program Specialist at the small interdisciplinary Carolina Health Informatics Program (CHIP), I designed, launched, and ran the DataAware summer program for Research Triangle-area high school students. The group of participating students, funded through a federal grant proposal I had written to create the program, came to campus each day for several weeks to study data science with a health care lens, both in a classroom setting and through project work with researchers affiliated with CHIP. While the first year of the program was success, I planned iterations based on student feedback (see poster below). Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the program was canceled in 2020, and we decided to offer it virtually in 2021. As part of the adaptation to the virtual setting, I collaborated with CHIP colleagues to develop a series of online training modules based on the classroom instruction sequence of the program. These were hosted in Jupyter Notebook, allowing students to complete coding exercises and run code snippets in line with the lesson.
