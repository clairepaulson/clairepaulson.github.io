About Me

Thanks for visiting my page! I’m currently the Instructional Design Team Lead at the UNC School of Government, where I get to work on many interesting projects that impact folks across North Carolina. Before I got into the world of education, I studied Cognitive Science in college, focusing on language acquisition, and went straight to a Ph.D. program to continue those studies – which I promptly dropped out of after realizing it wasn’t the field for me after all.

Fortunately, a teacher friend had gotten me interested in education, and I realized I could pursue my interest in how people learn outside of the lab instead. After developing curricula for a college access nonprofit and spending a few years as an educational program evaluator, I moved down to North Carolina, where I completed an MA in Educational Innovation, Entrepreneurship, and Technology at the UNC School of Education part-time while working full-time at the small interdisciplinary Carolina Health Informatics Program.

Outside of work, I love hiking in woods and mountains close to home and far away, trying out new tools at the UNC Makerspaces, solving and writing puzzles with my puzzlehunt team, and hanging out with my husband and cat.
